Officials

In order to play the sport. A referee is used to ensure the rules and laws of the game are being followed. They work together with the touch-judges to ensure the game is being played fairly and they keep track of the score. Equipped with a whistle, throughout the game a referee will make decisions that will dictate the flow of the game. For instance, calling advantage to let the game proceed or calling a penalty and stopping the flow of the game. In open play, the referee will at times verbally address players who are in danger of being penalised in an attempt to avoid disrupting the flow of the game. For example calling for players to get back onside or release the tackled player. In set plays such are the scrum they will control the position and timing of engagement in a scrum. If players are injured or at a high risk of injury they will stop the play reset the players. Referees have the power to issue yellow and red card in accordance with the laws of rugby. These card are normally given to player whose actions have put others at moderate or high risk of injury. The rules and laws of the game are absolute however the referee may identify circumstances that will increase or decrease the degree of a penalty.
