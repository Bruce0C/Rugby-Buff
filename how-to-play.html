Pitch

The rugby pitch is rectangular shaped and split into two halfâ€™s. A series of solid and dashed lined are used to mark distances and zones. There are goal posts on the opposite ends of the pitch and behind each post is a goal area. The goal are and post. Are used to score points. Grounding the ball in the goal area awards a team five points, after which a team is given the chance to kick the ball over the cross-bar of the posts the gain two more points. Positions

The icons in the image below represent each playing position numbered from one to fifteen. Positions fall into two groups, forwards and backs. Players are separated into these groups because of the roles their positions have in set pieces. These set pieces are scrums and line-outs. The image below shows how forwards and back can be positioned based on a scrum.
