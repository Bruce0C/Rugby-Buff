History

Rugby is a sport that came into being in 1823 England when a football player named William Webb Ellis decided to carry and run with a football instead of dribbling. In 1839, the first ever set of rules were established for the sport and referred to as the Cambridge Rules. Years later in 1871 the Rugby Football Union was founded to become the governing body of the sport. The first international match would then be played by England against Scotland.In 1895 the decision to pay players lead to formation of the Northern Union which consisted of twenty-two clubs. This union consisted of 22 club which years later in 1922 would became the professional rugby league level. Finally in 1995 rugby was recognised as a professional sport, allowing players at this level to be payed. Today rugby is played world wide and as a result inspired and encouraged millions of people to take part in all aspects. From spectators, coaches to officials and players.

Tournaments

World Cup

World Cup Named after the William Webb Ellis, the Web Ellis cup is the trophy used in the Rugby World Cup. The first World Cup tournament was hosted in New Zealand with Australia as co-hosts. 16 teams were divided into four pools, with the top two nations from each pool advancing to the quarterfinals. New Zealand who are referred to as The All Blacks were the winner of the first ever World Cup. To date, South Africa are the current World Cup champions. Winning the tournament for the second consecutive year. The next World Cup will be in 2027 with Australia as the host.

Six Nations

The Six nations is an annual tournament played in Europe consisting of Six teams. These teams include England, Ireland, Scotland, Wales, France and Italy.This tournament first took place in 1883 and was known The Home Nations as it only included nations within the United Kingdom. Following this, France joined the tournament in 1910, leading to the tournament being renamed The Five Nations. Finally in year 2000, Italy was added into the tournament leading to the tournament being renamed The Six Nations Championship. The next Guinness Six Nations Championship will be held in 2025 from February to March.

Autumn Nations

The Autumn-Nations tournament is an annual tournament.This tournament is relatively new, with the first Autumn Nations Cup being held in 2020. A total of eight teams take part in this tournament, these are England, Ireland, Scotland, Wales, France, Fiji, Italy, Georgia. The tournament is structured by dividing the teams into two groups of four. In 2024 it is now known as the Autumn Nations Series with fourteen teams taking part. With the addition of Japan, Portugal, Australia, South Africa, New Zealand. The current title winner for this series are South Africa and France who both went undefeated in their respective matches. The next series will take place on November 2025.
